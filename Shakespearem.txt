'Do swarm upon him--from the Western Isles Of kerns and gallowglasses is supplied And fortune, on his damned quarrel smiling Show'd like a rebel's whore: but all's too weak: For brave Macbeth--well he deserves that name--Disdaining fortune, with his brandish'd steel, Which smoked with bloody execution, Like valour's minion carved out his passage Till he faced the slave; Which ne'er shook hands, nor bade farewell to him, Till he unseam'd him from the nave to the chaps, And fix'd his head upon our battlements.
